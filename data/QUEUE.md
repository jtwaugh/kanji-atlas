Mind, nature, and Buddha-nature
仏性 — busshō (Ch. fóxìng) — Buddha-nature; the intrinsic awakened nature of all beings; SotozenWikipedia subject of Dōgen's radical reinterpretation ("all existence is Buddha-nature") in the Shōbōgenzō "Busshō" fascicle.
心 — shin / kokoro (Ch. xīn) — Mind; heart-mind; the fundamental locus of awareness, intention, and awakening in Zen discourse.
無心 — mushin (Ch. wúxīn) — "No-mind"; awareness free from fixation, clinging, and ego-attachment; Kanji Soul a central concept in Zen practice and aesthetics. Fabrizio Musacchio
真如 — shinnyo (Ch. zhēnrú) — Tathatā; "suchness" or "thusness"; the true nature of reality as it is, prior to and beyond all conceptual elaboration. Zen Buddha
法性 — hosshō (Ch. fǎxìng) — "Dharma-nature"; the intrinsic nature of all phenomena; title of a Shōbōgenzō fascicle.
自性 — jishō (Ch. zìxìng) — "Self-nature"; one's inherent essence, as invoked in the phrase 見性成仏 ("seeing self-nature, becoming Buddha").
身心一如 — shinjin ichinyo — "Body-mind as one"; the non-duality of physical and mental experience, Academia.edu foundational to Dōgen's embodied Zen.
即心是仏 — sokushin zebutsu — "This very mind is Buddha"; a cardinal Zen formula affirming that the presently functioning mind is itself Buddhahood Sotozen (Mumonkan Case 30; Blue Cliff Record Case 73).
平常心是道 — byōjōshin kore dō (Ch. píngcháng xīn shì dào) — "Ordinary mind is the Way"; attributed to Mazu Daoyi, expressing that the everyday mind — without contrivance — is the path itself Sotozen (Mumonkan Case 19).
仏心 — busshin (Ch. fóxīn) — "Buddha-mind"; the awakened mind; Dharma-rain Zen is called 仏心宗 (busshinshū), "the Buddha-mind school." Wikipedia
本心 — honshin — "Original mind"; the inherently pure, undefiled mind prior to the arising of delusion.
無常仏性 — mujō busshō — "Impermanence-is-Buddha-nature"; Dōgen's radical identification of impermanence itself with Buddha-nature in the "Busshō" fascicle.

Practice and meditation
坐禅 — zazen (Ch. zuòchán) — "Seated meditation"; the central practice of Zen Buddhism — sitting in meditative absorption. WikipediaZen-in-the-woods
只管打坐 — shikantaza — "Just sitting"; objectless, technique-free meditation that is the hallmark of Sōtō Zen; The Wisdom ExperienceMountainrainzen wholehearted sitting without seeking any particular state.
経行 — kinhin (Ch. jīngxíng) — "Walking meditation"; Empty Bowl Zendo slow, mindful walking Greatwave practiced between periods of seated zazen. Mbzc
摂心 — sesshin — "Gathering the mind"; an intensive meditation retreat, GreatwaveEmpty Bowl Zendo typically five to seven days One River Zen of near-continuous zazen.
安居 — ango — "Dwelling in peace"; an intensive communal training period Wikipedia (traditionally 90 days), originating in the Indian Buddhist rain-retreat. Treeleaf Zendo
三昧 — zanmai (Ch. sānmèi) — Samādhi; deep, one-pointed meditative absorption; concentrated, unified awareness.
自受用三昧 — jijuyū zanmai — "Self-receiving and self-employing samādhi"; Dōgen's term (from Bendōwa) for zazen as a self-fulfilling, self-authenticating meditative activity. SotozenThe Dewdrop
行持 — gyōji — "Continuous practice"; the sustained, ceaseless activity of the Buddhist Way; Sotozen subject of a major Shōbōgenzō fascicle.
弁道 — bendō — "Negotiating the Way"; wholehearted engagement in practice; title of Dōgen's foundational essay Bendōwa.
発菩提心 — hotsu bodaishin — "Arousing the mind of awakening"; generating bodhicitta, the essential first step of the Mahāyāna path, strongly emphasized by Dōgen.
伝法 — denpō — "Dharma transmission"; Wikipedia formal recognition of a disciple as a dharma heir, continuing the lineage from Śākyamuni Buddha. Mbzc
面授 — menju — "Face-to-face transmission"; the direct, intimate passing of dharma from master to disciple, Sotozen which Dōgen considered essential to authentic lineage.

Teacher-student encounter and dialogue
問答 — mondō (Ch. wèndá) — Formal question-and-answer exchange between master and student; the dialogic mode underlying most koan records.
提唱 — teishō — A master's formal commentary on a koan or Zen text, Greatwave understood as a demonstration of realized insight rather than an explanation.
独参 — dokusan — Private one-on-one interview with a Zen master Greatwave in which the student presents understanding of a koan; the core of Rinzai training.
参禅 — sanzen — "Going to Zen"; private interview with a master, One River ZenMbzc emphasizing the student's initiative in approaching the teacher.
上堂 — jōdō (Ch. shàngtáng) — "Ascending the hall"; formal convocation in which the abbot addresses the full assembly from the high seat, Mbzc sometimes engaging in mondō.
小参 — shōsan (Ch. xiǎocān) — "Informal assembly"; a less formal dharma dialogue in which the teacher offers a short talk and holds exchange with attendees.
法戦式 — hossenshiki — "Dharma combat ceremony"; ritualized public exchange of Zen questions and answers, typically marking the shuso's completion of a practice period.
室内 — shitsunai — "Within the room"; private, esoteric instruction given by a master; also denotes a lineage's body of koan teachings transmitted in the interview room.

Koans and encounter dialogue
公案 — kōan (Ch. gōng'àn) — "Public case"; a recorded dialogue, question, or episode from the Chan masters used as a focus for meditative inquiry to provoke direct insight beyond conceptual understanding. New World Encyclopedia +3
話頭 — watō (Ch. huàtóu) — "Head of the word"; the critical phrase or key point of a koan on which the practitioner concentrates during meditation; Patheos the basis of kānhuà (kanna) Zen.
看話禅 — kanna zen (Ch. kānhuà chán) — "Koan-introspecting Zen"; Sotozen meditation through sustained inquiry into a koan's critical phrase, Mbzc associated with the Linji/Rinzai lineage and systematized by Dahui Zonggao. Wikipedia
黙照禅 — mokushō zen (Ch. mòzhào chán) — "Silent illumination Zen"; Sotozen objectless sitting in quiet awareness, associated with the Caodong/Sōtō lineage and traced to Hongzhi Zhengjue.
着語 — jakugo (Ch. zhuóyǔ) — "Capping phrase"; a short verse or literary allusion appended to a koan passage to express or demonstrate insight; central to the Rinzai koan curriculum. Wikipedia
下語 — agyo (Ch. xiàyǔ) — "Appended words"; synonym of jakugo; standardized phrases students present as part of koan training, collected in phrase books such as the Zenrin Kushū.
拈提 — nentei (Ch. niāntí) — "Picking up and presenting"; a master's commentary that isolates and expounds the essential point of a koan. Mbzc
拈古 — nenko (Ch. niāngǔ) — "Picking up the old"; selecting an ancient case and adding commentary; the literary genre underlying koan collections.
本則 — honsoku (Ch. běnzé) — "Main case"; the core narrative or dialogue of a koan as presented in a collection, before verse and commentary.
頌古 — juko (Ch. sònggǔ) — "Verse on an old case"; poetic commentary appended to a koan's main case; Xuedou's 100 verses form the basis of the Blue Cliff Record. WikipediaThe Zen Universe
垂示 — suiji (Ch. chuíshì) — "Introductory pointer"; the prefatory remarks by the commentator (e.g., Yuanwu in the Blue Cliff Record) that orient the reader before the main case.
喝 — katsu (Ch. hè) — The shout used by Zen masters to shatter conceptual thinking; Linji Yixuan's signature teaching device, for which he distinguished four types.
棒 — bō (Ch. bàng) — The staff or stick wielded by a master as a teaching device; emblematic of Deshan Xuanjian, who declared "Thirty blows whether you speak or not."
拂子 — hossu (Ch. fúzǐ) — Fly-whisk; a short staff with bundled hair wielded ceremonially by a Zen master, symbolizing the authority to teach and transmit dharma.
疑団 — gidan — "Ball of doubt"; the concentrated mass of questioning and perplexity that builds during intense koan practice, fueling the drive toward breakthrough. Terebess Asia Online

Monastic structure and ranks
老師 — rōshi — "Venerable teacher"; a fully authorized Zen master who has completed training and received transmission; the primary title for a qualified teacher in Rinzai.
禅師 — zenji — "Zen master"; an honorific conferred upon great Zen ancestors, especially in Sōtō (e.g., Dōgen Zenji, Keizan Zenji).
和尚 — oshō (Ch. héshang) — "Preceptor"; a senior ordained Buddhist priest; Treeleaf Zendo also pronounced kashō (Tendai) or wajō (Shingon).
典座 — tenzo — "Head cook"; one of the six temple officers Zencenternorthshore (六知事 roku chiji); a position of high honor and the subject of Dōgen's Tenzo Kyōkun ("Instructions to the Cook").
維那 — ino — The officer responsible for the meditation hall, chanting services, and the monastic schedule; oversees the order and rhythm of communal life.
首座 — shuso — "Head seat"; the senior student for a practice period Zencenternorthshore (ango), who shares leadership with the guiding teacher and undergoes hossenshiki at its conclusion.
直日 — jikijitsu — The disciplinarian and timekeeper of the zendō; in Rinzai monasteries, second only to the rōshi in authority within the meditation hall.
侍者 — jisha — "Attendant"; the abbot's personal attendant, responsible for care of the teacher, serving tea, and directing students to dokusan. Mbzc
雲水 — unsui — "Clouds and water"; a Zen monk in training, evoking the image of drifting like clouds and flowing like water through the world.
禅堂 — zendō — "Zen hall"; the meditation hall and primary practice space in a Zen monastery.
僧堂 — sōdō — "Monks' hall"; the combined meditation, sleeping, and eating hall in a traditional Zen training monastery; the center of monastic life.
法堂 — hattō — "Dharma hall"; the hall where the abbot delivers formal lectures (jōdō, teishō) to the assembled community.
三門 — sanmon — "Triple gate" (also written 山門, "mountain gate"); the main entrance to a Zen monastery, Wikipedia symbolizing the three gates of liberation: emptiness, signlessness, and wishlessness.
印可 — inka — "Seal of approval"; the master's formal certification that a student has completed training and attained authentic realization.
嗣法 — shihō — "Dharma succession"; formal acknowledgment of a disciple as the master's successor in the lineage; the essential mechanism of Zen's transmission system. MbzcWikipedia

Death, time, and impermanence
無常 — mujō (Ch. wúcháng) — Impermanence; the foundational Buddhist teaching that all conditioned phenomena are transient; Dōgen identifies full realization of mujō with Buddha-nature itself. Association for Asian Studies
生死 — shōji (Ch. shēngsǐ) — "Birth-and-death"; saṃsāra; the cycle of transmigration; in Dōgen's Shōbōgenzō "Shōji" fascicle, liberation is found within — not apart from — birth-and-death.
輪廻 — rinne (Ch. lúnhuí) — The cycle of rebirth through the six realms; the round of existence from which awakening liberates.
末法 — mappō (Ch. mòfǎ) — "Latter dharma"; the degenerate age when the Buddha's teaching declines and true practice grows rare; a concept that shaped the Kamakura Buddhist milieu in which Dōgen wrote.
諸行無常 — shogyō mujō — "All compounded things are impermanent"; KOGEI STANDARD the first of the Three Dharma Seals (三法印 sanbōin). Terebess Asia Online
辞世 — jisei — "Departing the world"; a death poem composed by a Zen master at the point of death, expressing a final insight on life and impermanence.

Reality, emptiness, and non-duality
空 — kū (Ch. kōng) — Śūnyatā; "emptiness"; the absence of inherent, independent existence in all phenomena; Wikipedia the central insight of the Prajñāpāramitā literature, foundational for Zen.
縁起 — engi (Ch. yuánqǐ) — Pratītyasamutpāda; "dependent origination"; Terebess Asia Online all phenomena arise through interdependent causes and conditions — nothing exists in isolation.
不二 — funi (Ch. bù'èr) — "Not-two"; non-duality; subject and object, self and other, saṃsāra and nirvāṇa are ultimately not separate.
一如 — ichinyo (Ch. yīrú) — "Oneness-suchness"; the fundamental non-dual identity of all phenomena in their true nature.
色即是空 — shiki soku ze kū (Ch. sè jí shì kōng) — "Form is precisely emptiness"; the Heart Sutra's cardinal formula expressing the non-duality of phenomenal appearance and emptiness. ThaliraNew World Encyclopedia
法界 — hokkai (Ch. fǎjiè) — Dharmadhātu; "dharma realm"; the totality of all phenomena understood as a single, interpenetrating whole; a Huayan concept pervading Zen discourse.
事事無礙 — jijimuge (Ch. shìshì wú'ài) — "Unobstructed interpenetration of phenomenon with phenomenon"; Tibetan Buddhist Encyclopedia the highest of the Huayan Fourfold Dharmadhātu Wikipedia — each particular thing contains and reflects all others. Ancientdragon
不生 — fushō (Ch. bùshēng) — "Unborn"; the absolute reality in which there is no arising or perishing; Terebess Asia Online especially prominent in the teaching of Bankei Yōtaku.

Specific technical concepts
無 — mu (Ch. wú) — "No/nothing/without"; Zhaozhou's reply to "Does a dog have Buddha-nature?" — the first case of the Mumonkan Wikipedia and paradigmatic object of Rinzai meditation. Auckland Zen CentreFandom
理 — ri (Ch. lǐ) — "Principle"; the absolute, universal, undifferentiated aspect of reality; paired with 事 (ji) in the dialectic underlying the Five Ranks. Wikipedia
事 — ji (Ch. shì) — "Phenomena/events"; the particular, differentiated, concrete dimension of reality; the interplay of ri and ji structures much koan commentary.
身心脱落 — shinjin datsuraku — "Casting off body-mind"; Dōgen's term for the radical dropping away of dualistic consciousness, describing his pivotal awakening under Rujing at Mt. Tendō. Wikipedia
有時 — uji — "Being-time"; Dōgen's concept that existence and time are inseparable — all beings are time, all time is being; Feng Shui Source title of a key Shōbōgenzō fascicle. Wikipedia
現成公案 — genjōkōan — "The manifest fundamental point"; reality as the presently realized koan; Montereybayzencenter the first and most celebrated fascicle of the Shōbōgenzō. Sotozen
非思量 — hishiryō — "Non-thinking"; the mode of awareness in zazen that transcends both thinking (思量 shiryō) and not-thinking (不思量 fushiryō); Sotozen from Dōgen's Fukanzazengi.
修証一等 — shushō ittō — "Oneness of practice and realization"; Dōgen's cardinal principle that practice does not lead to enlightenment but is itself the expression of enlightenment. Sotozen
道得 — dōtoku — "Able to express the Way"; Dōgen's concept of authentic articulation of awakened understanding through words, actions, and silence. Sotozen
五位 — goi (Ch. wǔwèi) — "Five Ranks"; Dongshan Liangjie's framework of five relationships between absolute (正 shō) and relative (偏 hen), fundamental to both Sōtō and Rinzai koan curricula. Wikipedia
円相 — ensō — The Zen circle; a single brushstroke symbolizing absolute completeness, emptiness, and the cyclical nature of existence. Terebess Asia Online
行持道環 — gyōji dōkan — "The circle of the Way in continuous practice"; Dōgen's image of practice-realization as an unbroken, self-sustaining circle. Sotozen

Social and cultural concepts
社会 — shakai / shèhuì — "Society"; Meiji coinage of 社 (community) + 会 (assembly) to translate society — no prior Chinese compound carried this meaning; now one of the most fundamental terms in Chinese and Korean.
個人 — kojin / gèrén — "Individual"; Meiji coinage expressing the Western concept of the autonomous individual; adopted across East Asia.
文化 — bunka / wénhuà — "Culture"; classical Chinese compound (originally "civilizing through literature") re-coined to translate English/German culture / Kultur.
文明 — bunmei / wénmíng — "Civilization"; classical Chinese compound re-coined to translate civilization, popularized by Fukuzawa Yukichi in his Bunmeiron no Gairyaku (1875).
教育 — kyōiku / jiàoyù — "Education"; given its modern pedagogical meaning in Meiji Japan to translate the Western concept of systematic education.
宗教 — shūkyō / zōngjiào — "Religion"; Meiji coinage of 宗 (sect) + 教 (teaching), creating the Western category of religion as a domain distinct from philosophy and custom.
自由 — jiyū / zìyóu — "Freedom / liberty"; classical Chinese and Buddhist term (meaning "as one wishes") re-coined by Fukuzawa Yukichi and others to translate liberty in Western political philosophy.
階級 — kaikyū / jiējí — "Social class"; classical compound re-coined for the modern sociological and Marxist concept of class.
衛生 — eisei / wèishēng — "Hygiene / public health"; classical Zhuangzi term ("guarding life") radically re-coined by Nagayo Sensai (c. 1872) to translate German Gesundheitspflege; given an entirely new public-health meaning.
演説 — enzetsu / yǎnshuō — "Public speech / oration"; coined by Fukuzawa Yukichi, who also introduced 討論 (tōron, "debate") alongside it.
広告 — kōkoku / guǎnggào — "Advertisement"; Meiji coinage, 広 (wide) + 告 (inform); now standard in Chinese and Korean.
理想 — risō / lǐxiǎng — "Ideal"; Meiji coinage of 理 (reason) + 想 (thought) to translate ideal.
目的 — mokuteki / mùdì — "Purpose / objective"; Meiji coinage of 目 (eye/target) + 的 (mark) to translate purpose.

Arts and humanities concepts
美術 — bijutsu / měishù — "Fine arts / visual arts"; Meiji coinage translating Bildende Kunst ("fine arts"); now standard in Chinese and Korean.
文学 — bungaku / wénxué — "Literature"; classical compound given its modern academic meaning as a discipline through Meiji usage.
芸術 — geijutsu / yìshù — "Art"; re-coined for the Western aesthetic concept of art as distinct from mere craft; now standard in Chinese and Korean.
歴史 — rekishi / lìshǐ — "History"; classical characters re-coined as a compound to denote the modern academic discipline; standard across East Asia.
心理学 — shinrigaku / xīnlǐxué — "Psychology"; Meiji compound, 心 (mind) + 理 (principle) + 学 (study), to translate psychology.
悲劇 — higeki / bēijù — "Tragedy" (the dramatic genre); Meiji coinage of 悲 (sorrow) + 劇 (drama) to translate the Western theatrical category.
喜劇 — kigeki / xǐjù — "Comedy" (the dramatic genre); Meiji counterpart to 悲劇; now standard across East Asia.
